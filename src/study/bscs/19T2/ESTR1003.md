## 课程简介 (What is this course about?)

**ESTR1003 - 工程物理：电磁学、光学与现代物理** 是 PHYS1110 的延续，深入探讨电磁现象、光学原理和量子物理基础。这门课揭示了从经典物理到现代物理的跨越，是理解当代科技的钥匙。

**课程定位**：
- 面向工程学院理科学生（ESTR 系列）
- PHYS1110（力学与热力学）的后续课程
- 为电子工程、信息工程等专业奠定物理基础

**知识在物理大厦中的位置**：
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│     量子力学、相对论、粒子物理        │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ← ESTR1003: 电磁学、光学、量子 →    │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│     PHYS1110: 经典力学与热力学       │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│        初等物理、数学基础             │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**核心内容**：
- **电磁学**：电场、磁场、麦克斯韦方程组
- **光学**：干涉、衍射、偏振
- **现代物理**：量子理论、原子物理、固体物理

**未来发展方向**：
- 电磁学 → 无线通信、天线设计
- 光学 → 光纤通信、激光技术
- 量子物理 → 量子计算、半导体器件

## 📚 课程笔记 (Course Notes)

#### **Topic 1: Electrostatics (静电学)**
This topic establishes the foundational concepts of electric charges and fields.

**1.1 Electric Charge (电荷)**
- **Basic Concepts**: Properties of electric charges, conductors and insulators, semiconductors, and superconductors
- **Coulomb's Law**: The fundamental law describing electrostatic forces between point charges
  - Formula: F = k·q₁q₂/r²
  - Understanding the inverse-square relationship
- **Shell Theorem**: Key theorem about charge distribution in spherical shells
- **Charge Quantization**: The fact that charge exists in discrete multiples of elementary charge e

**1.2 Electric Fields (电场)**
- Definition and visualization of electric fields
- Electric field lines and their properties
- Electric field due to point charges and continuous charge distributions
- Electric dipole behavior in uniform fields
- **Application**: Microwave oven heating mechanism (dipole rotation)

**1.3 Gauss's Law (高斯定律)**
- Concept of electric flux
- Gauss's Law and its applications to symmetric charge distributions
- **Applications**:
  - Infinite line charge
  - Infinite planar charge distribution
  - Spherical shell charge distribution
- Faraday's cage principle

**1.4 Electric Potential (电势)**
- Conservative nature of electrostatic force
- Electric potential energy vs. electric potential
- Equipotential surfaces
- Calculating potential from electric field and vice versa
- Method of images (imaging method)

**1.5 Capacitance (电容)**
- Definition of capacitance
- Capacitors in parallel and series
- Energy stored in electric fields
- Dielectrics and their effects
- Supercapacitors and modern applications

**Key Difficulties in Topic 1:**
- Understanding the vector nature of electric fields
- Applying Gauss's Law to various symmetric charge distributions
- Converting between electric field and potential
- Understanding the physical meaning of gradient operations

#### **Topic 2: Magnetostatics (静磁学)**
This topic explores magnetic fields produced by currents and the forces on moving charges.

**2.1 Magnetic Field (磁场)**
- Magnetic poles and the non-existence of magnetic monopoles
- Definition of magnetic field B
- Magnetic force on a moving charged particle: F = qv × B
- **Applications**:
  - Mass spectrometer
  - Cyclotron and particle accelerators
  - Earth's radiation belt (Van Allen belt)

**2.2 Magnetic Fields Due to Currents (电流产生的磁场)**
- Biot-Savart Law
- Magnetic field due to:
  - Long straight wire
  - Circular current loop
  - Solenoid
  - Toroid
- Ampere's Law and its applications
- Force between parallel currents

**2.3 Induction and Inductance (感应与电感)**
- Faraday's Law of Induction
- Lenz's Law (understanding the direction of induced current)
- Self-induction and mutual induction
- RL circuits
- Energy stored in magnetic fields
- Transformers and power transmission
- **Key Application**: Why high voltage transmission?

**Key Difficulties in Topic 2:**
- Understanding the right-hand rule for magnetic field directions
- Distinguishing between magnetic force, field, and flux
- Applying Ampere's Law correctly
- Understanding the physical meaning of displacement current
- Comprehending electromagnetic induction and its applications

#### **Topic 3: Electromagnetic Waves and Optics (电磁波与光学)**
This topic connects electricity, magnetism, and light through Maxwell's equations.

**3.1 Maxwell's Equations (麦克斯韦方程组)**
- The four fundamental equations:
  - Gauss's Law for electricity
  - Gauss's Law for magnetism
  - Faraday's Law
  - Ampere-Maxwell Law
- Differential form of Maxwell's equations
- Understanding divergence and curl operators

**3.2 Electromagnetic Waves (电磁波)**
- Discovery that light is an electromagnetic wave
- Plane electromagnetic waves
- Speed of light: c = 1/√(ε₀μ₀)
- Electromagnetic spectrum
- Polarization of EM waves:
  - Linear polarization
  - Circular polarization
  - Elliptical polarization
- Poynting vector and wave intensity
- Radiation pressure

**3.3 Wave Propagation at Interfaces (界面处的波传播)**
- Reflection and transmission coefficients
- Snell's Law of refraction
- Brewster's angle
- Total internal reflection
- **Application**: Optical fibers and the work of Prof. Charles Kao (CUHK Nobel Laureate)

**3.4 Images and Optics (成像与光学)**
- Real vs. virtual images
- Plane mirrors
- Spherical mirrors (concave and convex)
- Mirror equation: 1/p + 1/i = 1/f
- Magnification
- Refraction at spherical surfaces
- Diffraction limit

**Key Difficulties in Topic 3:**
- Understanding the symmetry and complementary nature of Maxwell's equations
- Visualizing electromagnetic wave propagation
- Understanding polarization states
- Applying boundary conditions at interfaces
- Deriving and using Snell's Law
- Understanding total internal reflection conditions

### Additional Topics

#### **Topic 4: Interference and Diffraction (干涉与衍射)**

**4.1 Interference (干涉)**
- **Young's Double-Slit Experiment**: Proved light is a wave (1801)
- **Constructive Interference**: d sinθ = mλ (m = 0, 1, 2,...)
- **Destructive Interference**: d sinθ = (m + ½)λ
- **Coherence**: Temporal and spatial coherence requirements

**4.2 Thin Film Interference (薄膜干涉)**
- Phase shifts upon reflection (π shift when n increases)
- Applications: Anti-reflection coatings, soap bubbles, colored coatings
- **Example**: MgF₂ coating on camera lenses (λ/4 thickness)

**4.3 Optical Interferometers (光学干涉仪)**
- Michelson Interferometer
- Mach-Zehnder Interferometer
- Fabry-Perot Interferometer
- **Application**: LIGO gravitational wave detection (4km arms, measuring changes 10,000× smaller than atomic nucleus)

**4.4 Diffraction (衍射)**
- **Huygens' Principle**: Each point on wavefront acts as point source
- **Single-Slit Diffraction**: a sinθ = mλ (minima)
- **Double-Slit Diffraction**: Combined interference + diffraction pattern
- **Diffraction Grating**: d sinθ = mλ, high resolution for spectroscopy

**4.5 Resolution Limit (分辨率极限)**
- **Rayleigh's Criterion**: θ_R = 1.22λ/D (circular aperture)
- **Diffraction Limit**: Resolution ~ λ/2
- **Applications**: Telescope resolution, microscope imaging limits

#### **Topic 5: Quantum Theory and Atomic Physics (量子理论与原子物理)**

**5.1 Photons and Matter Waves (光子与物质波)**
- **Photoelectric Effect**: Einstein's explanation (1905)
  - Energy: E = hf (h = Planck's constant)
  - Work function Φ: minimum energy to escape metal
  - K_max = hf - Φ
  - **Key insight**: Light intensity affects photon number, NOT photon energy
- **Compton Effect**: Photon momentum p = h/λ
  - Compton shift: Δλ = (h/mc)(1 - cosφ)

**5.2 de Broglie Matter Waves (德布罗意物质波)**
- **de Broglie Hypothesis**: λ = h/p for all particles
- **Experimental Confirmation**: Electron diffraction (Davisson-Germer)
- **Applications**: Electron microscopy (resolution ~0.01 nm)

**5.3 Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle (海森堡不确定性原理)**
- Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2
- **Physical Meaning**: Cannot simultaneously know position and momentum
- **Applications**: Quantum confinement, zero-point energy

**5.4 Atomic Structure (原子结构)**
- **Bohr Model of Hydrogen**:
  - Quantized orbits: r_n = a₀n² (a₀ = 0.529 Å)
  - Energy levels: E_n = -13.6 eV/n²
  - Transition: hf = E_high - E_low
- **Hydrogen Spectrum**:
  - Lyman series (n→1): UV
  - Balmer series (n→2): Visible
  - Paschen series (n→3): IR

**5.5 Quantum Numbers and Pauli Exclusion (量子数与泡利原理)**
- Principal quantum number n
- Orbital quantum number l (0→s, 1→p, 2→d, 3→f)
- Magnetic quantum number m_l
- Spin quantum number m_s (±½)
- **Pauli Exclusion Principle**: No two electrons can have same quantum numbers

**5.6 Magnetic Resonance (磁共振)**
- NMR原理: Spin flip in magnetic field
- **Applications**: MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging), Chemical analysis

#### **Topic 6: Conduction of Electricity in Solids (固体导电)**

**6.1 Energy Bands (能带)**
- **Crystalline Solids**: Atoms arranged in periodic lattice
- **Band Formation**: Discrete energy levels split into bands due to Pauli principle
- **Band Gap**: Energy gap between valence band and conduction band

**6.2 Classification of Solids (固体分类)**
- **Metals**: Fermi level in middle of band, high conductivity
  - Fermi energy E_F: energy with 50% occupancy at T=0
  - Fermi-Dirac statistics: P(E) = 1/(e^(E-E_F)/kT + 1)
- **Insulators**: Large band gap (>5 eV), no conduction
- **Semiconductors**: Small band gap (~1 eV)
  - Intrinsic: Equal electrons and holes
  - Resistivity: ρ = m/(ne²τ)

**6.3 Semiconductor Doping (半导体掺杂)**
- **n-type**: Donor impurities (Group V: P, As) → extra electrons
- **p-type**: Acceptors (Group III: B, Al) → holes
- **Doping effect**: Increase carrier concentration by factor of 10⁶ with only 1/5,000,000 replacement!

**6.4 p-n Junction (p-n结)**
- **Formation**: Diffusion creates depletion region
- **Built-in Potential**: V₀ at junction
- **Rectifier Action**: Forward bias conducts, reverse bias blocks
- **I-V Characteristics**: Exponential current-voltage relationship

**6.5 Modern Applications (现代应用)**
- **LED (Light Emitting Diode)**: Electron-hole recombination emits photons
  - Color determined by band gap: λ = hc/E_g
  - Blue LED Nobel Prize 2014
- **Solar Cells**: Photon absorption creates electron-hole pairs
  - Power conversion efficiency: 15-20%
- **Transistor (MOSFET)**: Three-terminal semiconductor amplifier
  - Basis of all modern electronics
  - Moore's Law: Transistor count doubles every 18-24 months

### Exam Preparation Tips

1. **For Electrostatics**: Practice calculating electric fields and potentials using both direct integration and Gauss's Law
2. **For Magnetostatics**: Master the right-hand rules for magnetic field directions
3. **For Induction**: Focus on understanding Lenz's Law direction and energy conservation
4. **For EM Waves**: Understand the relationship between E and B fields, and practice polarization problems
5. **For Optics**: Be comfortable with Snell's Law, mirror equations, and critical angle calculations
6. **For Quantum Physics**: Understand photoelectric effect equations, de Broglie wavelength, and Bohr model energy levels
8. **For Solid State**: Know the differences between metals, semiconductors, and insulators; understand p-n junction behavior
